## Design Context

### Users

Primary: Technical recruiters doing fast scans (90-second decision window). Secondary: peers/collaborators evaluating craft depth. Tertiary: senior leaders/execs assessing strategic fit and non-technical recruiters who need signals without jargon. The interface must serve fast scanners AND reward deep explorers — a dual-mode challenge.

### Core Message (90-second takeaway)

Kyle is the complete package for CS/AI Ops leadership: strategic thinker + technical executor + customer relationship builder. Bonus signal: fast learner who can acquire whatever skills are needed. This is NOT "I know AI tools" — it's "I operationalize AI into customer outcomes with documented evidence."

### Brand Personality

The tension to resolve: sharp/precise/technical AND warm/collaborative/human AND bold/innovative AND refined/strategic. The synthesis: **Confident without arrogance. Substantive without being dense. Human without being casual.** Think a thoughtful operator who's also an early adopter — not a consultant, not a startup bro, not a resume-bot.

### Anti-References (MUST AVOID)

1. **Generic SaaS startup template** (CRITICAL) — indigo/purple gradients, glassmorphism cards, hero metric tiles, gradient text. This is the #1 failure mode. It must not look AI-generated or templated.
2. Overly corporate/stuffy Big4 brochure
3. Design-flex creative portfolio (wrong audience)
4. Plain developer resume site (forgettable)

### Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion** — Show the work, the rigor, the numbers. Don't claim "strategic thinker" — demonstrate it through structure.
2. **Scannable at speed, rewarding at depth** — 90-second path must be crystal clear; deeper exploration should reveal more substance.
3. **Distinctly human** — Typography, copy, and layout choices should signal a real person with taste, not a template filled in.
4. **Confident restraint** — Visual hierarchy through space and weight, not effects. No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no hero metric tiles.
5. **One clear step ahead** — The portfolio should feel like it was designed by someone who knows what's coming in AI Ops, not chasing trends.
